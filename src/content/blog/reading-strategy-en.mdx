## Reading Section Structure

The TCF Canada reading section has 39 questions in 60 minutes. Questions are ordered by difficulty:

| Level | Questions | Text Type | Points/Q |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| A1 | 1-4 | Signs, notices, simple ads | 3 |
| A2 | 5-10 | Short messages, emails, instructions | 9 |
| B1 | 11-19 | News articles, product descriptions | 15 |
| B2 | 20-30 | Opinion pieces, editorials, reports | 21 |
| C1 | 31-35 | Professional articles, academic reviews | 27 |
| C2 | 36-39 | Literary works, in-depth analysis | 33 |

## Strategy by Level

### A1-A2 (Questions 1-10): Zero Mistakes

These texts are short — everyday signs, ads, or messages. Keyword matching is most effective:

- Read the question first
- Find the matching keyword in the text
- The answer is usually right next to it

> Use [HiTCF Level Practice](/en/tests) to drill A1-A2 questions specifically.

### B1-B2 (Questions 11-30): The Main Battlefield

These 20 questions make up most of your score. Texts are longer and require understanding context:

1. **Read the question first** — know what you're looking for
2. **Locate the key sentence** — each answer maps to a specific part of the text
3. **Eliminate obviously wrong options** before choosing

> [Open-book mode](/en/tests) lets you see the answer and key sentence first, then practice to reinforce.

### C1-C2 (Questions 31-39): Get What You Can

High-level texts are long and abstract. For CLB 7+, you don't need them all right:

- Getting 3/5 on C1 is great
- Getting 1-2 on C2 is a bonus
- **Don't spend too much time on C2** — securing B1-B2 matters more

## The 3-Step Practice Method

### Step 1: Open-Book Review

Open [open-book mode](/en/tests) and for each question:
1. Read the trilingual translation to understand the question
2. Check the key sentence to see where the answer comes from
3. Save unfamiliar words to your [vocabulary notebook](/en/vocabulary)

### Step 2: Practice Mode

With familiarity, your accuracy improves significantly. Wrong answers go to your [review book](/en/review) automatically.

### Step 3: Review Book Focus

Questions you got wrong even after seeing the answer = your real weak spots. Focus here for 3x efficiency.

## How Many Right for CLB 7+?

Reading CLB 7 requires roughly **453-498 points** (~25-28 correct out of 39). Maintain 80%+ on B1-B2 plus a few C1 questions, and you'll make it.
